=================================================================

[FIXED] Geocoding bug in centroids.csv corrupted ~74% of corridor distances.

       Problem  : The original centroids.csv was geocoded by area NAME, which
                  collapsed 66 of 134 CFS areas onto shared, often-wrong
                  coordinates. Buffalo, Rochester and Albany all sat on New
                  York City's lat/lon; Baltimore, MD landed in Columbia, SC.
                  This produced 118 zero-distance corridors and silently wrong
                  distances on 13,266 of 17,822 corridors (74%).

       Fix      : Coordinates rebuilt from the authoritative Census internal
                  points (INTPTLAT/INTPTLON) carried in 2022_CFS_Areas.shp,
                  joined on GEO_ID (shapefile field CFS22_GE_1). All 134 areas
                  now have distinct, correct coordinates.

       Rebuilt  : 05_features\centroids.csv          (coords corrected)
                  05_features\distance_matrix.csv    (17,822 haversine dists; 0 zeros)
                  05_features\features_master.csv    (8 geo columns recomputed)
                  05_features\port_proximity.csv     (5 port columns recomputed)
       Backups  : same folder, *.orig.csv (pre-fix versions)
       Scripts  : scripts\rebuild_centroids.py -> rebuild_features.py
                  (see scripts\README.md for the full pipeline)

       Impact   : Network Graph + Rerouting workstreams must re-pull the four
                  rebuilt files. Non-geographic columns (VAL, TON, norms,
                  vulnerability_score, rank, commodity fields) are UNCHANGED.
